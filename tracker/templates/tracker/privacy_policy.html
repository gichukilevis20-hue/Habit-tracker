Privacy policy

How your habit data is collected and used

This page explains the data the app stores, why it stores it, and what the expectations are around uploads, notifications, exports, and donation records.

What we collect

The app stores account details such as username, email address, gender, age, theme preferences, fonts, optional profile images, habit definitions, daily entries, weekly review notes, uploaded review photos, and donation records including method, amount, and payment reference details.

Why we collect it

Profile settings personalize the interface. Habit and review data power the dashboard, charts, calendar, reminders, and exports. Donation data supports contribution history and admin visibility for project support activity.

Storage and retention

Application data is stored in the configured database. Uploaded profile images and review photos are stored in the media directory configured for the project. In local development this project uses SQLite and a local media folder unless you replace them with production services.

Email reminders and notifications

If email sending is enabled, the app may send personalized missed-habit reminders and weekly review prompts to the address attached to the account. Those reminders can include the habit title, goal details, and the reason you wrote down for wanting the habit so the message reflects your own data rather than generic copy. In local development, the default email backend writes those messages to the console rather than sending them externally.

Payments and donations

Donation records may include M-Pesa phone numbers, Bitcoin wallet destinations, Lightning invoices, transaction references, statuses, and timestamps. If Daraja credentials are configured, M-Pesa requests can be forwarded to Safaricom’s Daraja API. Otherwise the project falls back to a local development-safe placeholder flow.

Sharing and access

The application is designed not to share personal data by default. Staff users and Django admins may access user, habit, review, and donation data to maintain the system and monitor usage. If you deploy the app publicly, you should document who has staff access and why.

Your controls

Users can update their profile information, change themes and fonts, upload or replace photos, delete habits, and export their stored habit records as CSV or text reports from the profile area.

Deployment responsibilities

For production use, protect this app with HTTPS, strong secret management, secure media storage, restricted admin access, clear backup policies, and a retention policy appropriate to your users and local regulations.
